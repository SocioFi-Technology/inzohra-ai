# BV Drafter Few-Shot Examples — Santa Rosa Building Division

Each example follows the BV comment-letter dialect:
`Sheet <X>: <observation>. <Code> §<section> requires <requirement>. <Severity>: <action>.`

---

## Example 1 — Architectural / Egress Window
**Finding (rule: AR-EGRESS-WIN-001, severity: revise)**
Sheet A-1.2: The egress window at Bedroom 2 has a net clear opening area of 4.2 sq ft per the window schedule. CRC §R310.2.1 requires a minimum net clear opening of 5.7 sq ft for sleeping rooms. Revise: update the window schedule and plans to provide a compliant emergency escape and rescue opening with a net clear area of not less than 5.7 sq ft.

---

## Example 2 — Architectural / Egress Window Height
**Finding (rule: AR-WIN-HEIGHT-001, severity: revise)**
Sheet A-1.2: Window tag W-03 at the master bedroom shows a net clear opening height of 18 inches per the window schedule. CRC §R310.2.2 requires the minimum net clear opening height to be 24 inches. Revise: provide a window with a net clear opening height of not less than 24 inches, or replace with a code-compliant egress window assembly.

---

## Example 3 — Accessibility / Accessible Route Width
**Finding (rule: ACC-ROUTE-WIDTH-001, severity: revise)**
Sheet A-1.1: The accessible route corridor connecting the accessible parking stall to the primary building entrance measures 34 inches clear width per the floor plan dimensions. CBC §11B-403.5.1 requires accessible routes to provide a minimum clear width of 36 inches. Revise: widen the corridor to not less than 36 inches clear width and dimension the compliant route on the revised plans.

---

## Example 4 — Accessibility / Restroom Turning Radius
**Finding (rule: ACC-TURN-RAD-001, severity: revise)**
Sheet A-3.1: The accessible restroom shown on the floor plan does not provide a 60-inch diameter turning space clear of all obstructions within the room. CBC §11B-304.3.1 requires a 60-inch minimum diameter turning circle or a T-shaped turning space in accessible restrooms. Revise: reconfigure the restroom layout to provide the required turning space and dimension it on the plans.

---

## Example 5 — Mechanical / Combustion Air
**Finding (rule: ME-COMB-AIR-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet M-1.0: The plans show a gas-fired furnace rated at 125,000 BTUH in a confined equipment closet but do not indicate the source or size of combustion air openings. CRC §R303.4.1 requires that combustion air be provided in accordance with the appliance manufacturer's installation instructions and the applicable fuel gas code. Provide: add combustion-air opening sizes, locations, and calculation to the mechanical plans or specifications.

---

## Example 6 — Electrical / Panel Schedule
**Finding (rule: EL-PANEL-SCH-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet E-1.0: The electrical plans do not include a panel schedule identifying circuit breaker sizes, circuit descriptions, conductor sizes, and loads for the proposed 200-ampere service panel. CEC §408.4(A) requires that each switchboard, switchgear, or panelboard be legibly marked in the field with a circuit directory indicating the purpose of each overcurrent device. Provide: include a complete panel schedule on the electrical drawings.

---

## Example 7 — Plumbing / Water Heater Seismic Strapping
**Finding (rule: PL-WH-SEISMIC-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet P-1.0: The plans show a 50-gallon gas water heater but do not detail the seismic restraint strapping required in Seismic Design Category D. CPC §507.2 requires water heaters to be anchored or strapped to resist horizontal displacement due to earthquake motion. Provide: add a detail or note specifying seismic strapping at the upper one-third and lower one-third of the water heater in conformance with FEMA P-50 or DSA/OSHPD requirements.

---

## Example 8 — Fire / Smoke Alarm Location
**Finding (rule: FL-SMOKE-LOC-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet A-1.1: The floor plan does not indicate the location of smoke alarms in all sleeping rooms, immediately outside each sleeping room, and on each additional story of the dwelling. CRC §R314.3 requires smoke alarms to be installed in the specific locations listed in that section. Provide: show the location of all required smoke alarms on the floor plans with a note referencing CRC §R314.

---

## Example 9 — Energy / CF-1R Form
**Finding (rule: EN-CF1R-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet T24-1: The submittal does not include a completed CF-1R (Certificate of Compliance — Residential) for the proposed addition. Title 24, Part 6 §10-103(a)(1) requires that a Certificate of Compliance be included with the plans when submitted for permit. Provide: include a current CalCERTS- or CHEERS-registered CF-1R form for the scope of work and attach it to the plan set.

---

## Example 10 — Fire / Egress Door Hardware
**Finding (rule: FL-EGRESS-HW-001, severity: revise)**
Sheet A-2.1: The door schedule indicates lever-operated hardware on Door D-05, which is designated as an egress door from a room with an occupant load exceeding 49. CBC §1010.1.9 requires egress doors serving a room with an occupant load greater than 49 to be equipped with panic hardware or fire exit hardware. Revise: specify listed panic hardware or fire exit hardware on Door D-05 and any other egress doors serving occupant loads above the threshold.

---

## Example 11 — Structural / Hold-Down Not Specified
**Finding (rule: ST-HOLDDOWN-001, severity: provide)**
Sheet S-1.1: The shear wall schedule references hold-down hardware at the end posts of SW-1 through SW-4, but no hold-down product designation, size, or ICBO/ICC-ES report number is indicated on the structural drawings or specifications. CBC §2308.6.8.1 requires hold-down connectors to be shown on the plans with the type, size, and installation requirements sufficient for the building official to verify compliance. Provide: specify the hold-down hardware product, capacity, and applicable ICC-ES evaluation report number on the structural plans.

---

## Example 12 — CalGreen / Construction Waste Management
**Finding (rule: CG-WASTE-001, severity: provide)**
Project-wide: The submittal does not include a Construction Waste Management Plan documenting how a minimum of 65 percent of construction waste will be diverted from landfill disposal. CALGreen §4.408.1 requires a completed waste management plan to be submitted with the permit application for new residential construction and additions over 1,000 sq ft. Provide: submit a Construction Waste Management Plan identifying the intended diversion methods, waste haulers, and recycling facilities prior to permit issuance.
