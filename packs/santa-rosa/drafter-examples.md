# Santa Rosa BV Drafter Examples

BV comment-letter dialect for the City of Santa Rosa Building Division, 2022 California code cycle.
Format: `Sheet <X>: <observation>. <Code> §<section> requires <requirement>. <Severity>: <action>.`

All comments open with the sheet reference. Code section is cited inline. Severity keyword closes the action.
Round 1 typography: italic. BV Job No. format: SR-XXXX-YY.

## Architectural

### Example 01 — Egress window NCO area (Revise)
**Draft input:** Bedroom 2 egress window NCO 4.2 sqft, code requires 5.7 sqft.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.2: The egress window at Bedroom 2 (tag W-04) has a net clear opening area of 4.2 sq ft per the window schedule. CRC §R310.2.1 requires a minimum net clear opening area of 5.7 sq ft for sleeping rooms. Revise: update the window schedule and floor plans to provide a compliant emergency escape and rescue opening with a net clear area of not less than 5.7 sq ft. Dimension the net clear width and height on the revised window schedule.

### Example 02 — Egress window sill height (Revise)
**Draft input:** Bedroom 3 egress window sill at 52 inches AFF, code max is 44 inches.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.2: Bedroom 3 shows egress window tag W-07 with a sill height of 52 inches above the finish floor per the floor plan dimensions. CRC §R310.2.3 requires the bottom of the net clear opening to be not more than 44 inches above the finish floor. Revise: lower the sill height to not more than 44 inches AFF, or provide an approved window well and ladder assembly in accordance with CRC §R310.2.3.1, and update the window schedule accordingly.

### Example 03 — Door landing missing (Revise)
**Draft input:** Front entry door no landing shown, floor drops 2 inches immediately outside.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.1: The front entry door (D-01) on the west elevation does not show the required landing on the exterior side of the door. The finish grade falls approximately 2 inches below the threshold within the first 12 inches outside the door. CRC §R311.3.1 requires a landing with a minimum 36-inch depth in the direction of travel on each side of the exterior door. Revise: provide a landing not less than 36 inches deep and not less than the door width on the exterior side of D-01 and show it dimensioned on the site plan and floor plan.

### Example 04 — Stair riser height inconsistency (Revise)
**Draft input:** Stair S-01 risers measured at 6.8 inches by scale but schedule says 7.75 inches.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-2.1: The stair section at Stair S-01 shows a riser height of 6.8 inches per scaled dimensions, which conflicts with the stair schedule notation of 7.75 inches. CRC §R311.7.5.1 requires stair risers to be uniform with a maximum height of 7-3/4 inches and a minimum height of 4 inches. Clarify: revise the stair section and schedule to show consistent, dimensioned riser heights. If the actual riser is 7.75 inches, confirm this does not exceed the CRC maximum and recheck the run-to-riser ratio per CRC §R311.7.5.2.

### Example 05 — Handrail continuity missing (Revise)
**Draft input:** Stair handrail shown only on one side, four risers, no return noted.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-2.1: The stair section at Stair S-01 shows a handrail on the left side only, with no graspable return shown at the top and bottom of the stair flight. CRC §R311.7.8.2 requires handrails to return to the wall, post, or newel within 12 inches of the landing and at the top of the stair. Revise: show the handrail return details at both the top and bottom terminations, or provide a newel post meeting the geometrical requirements of CRC §R311.7.8.3. Dimension the graspable profile on the detail sheet.

### Example 06 — Smoke barrier missing at attached garage (Revise)
**Draft input:** No 5/8-inch type X drywall noted at garage-to-house common wall.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.1: The floor plan does not specify the required fire separation at the common wall between the attached garage and the dwelling unit. CRC §R302.5.1 requires not less than 5/8-inch Type X gypsum board on the garage side of the wall separating the dwelling unit from the garage. Revise: add a keynote or general note to the plans specifying 5/8-inch Type X gypsum board on the garage side of all walls and the ceiling of the garage where the garage is located below a habitable space, consistent with CRC §R302.5.1.

### Example 07 — Habitable room minimum ceiling height (Clarify)
**Draft input:** Bonus room shows 7-foot 2-inch ceiling height but sloped ceiling covers >1/3 of room.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-2.1: The bonus room ceiling section shows a minimum clear height of 7 feet 2 inches, with a sloped portion that appears to cover more than one-third of the room area. CRC §R305.1 requires habitable rooms to have a minimum ceiling height of 7 feet 0 inches over at least one-half of the floor area (beams and girders may project to within 6 feet 8 inches). Clarify: provide a reflected ceiling plan or annotated cross-section for the bonus room that documents the percentage of floor area at or above each ceiling height, confirming compliance with CRC §R305.1.

## Accessibility

### Example 08 — Accessible route clear width (Revise)
**Draft input:** Accessible corridor measured 34 inches clear between base cabinets and opposite wall.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.1: The accessible route corridor connecting the accessible parking stall to the primary building entrance measures 34 inches clear width per the floor plan dimensions. CBC §11B-403.5.1 requires a minimum clear width of 36 inches along accessible routes. Revise: widen the corridor to not less than 36 inches clear, free of obstructions, and dimension the compliant accessible route on the revised floor plan.

### Example 09 — Restroom turning radius (Revise)
**Draft input:** Accessible restroom lacks 60-inch diameter turning circle.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-3.1: The accessible restroom on the first floor does not provide a 60-inch diameter turning space free of all obstructions within the room. CBC §11B-304.3.1 requires a 60-inch minimum diameter turning circle or a T-shaped turning space in accessible toilet rooms. Revise: reconfigure the accessible restroom layout to provide the required clear turning space and dimension it on the plans. The turning space may overlap the required maneuvering clearances at the toilet, lavatory, and door.

### Example 10 — Van-accessible stall not provided (Provide)
**Draft input:** Accessible parking shows 5 accessible stalls, no van stall indicated.
**Polished output:** Sheet C-1.0: The site plan shows five (5) accessible parking stalls but does not designate any stall as van-accessible with the required 8-foot access aisle. CBC §11B-502.2 requires at least one van-accessible parking space for every six or fraction of six accessible spaces provided. A van-accessible space requires a minimum 11-foot width stall or a 5-foot-wide marked access aisle adjacent to a standard accessible stall. Provide: designate and dimension a van-accessible stall, including the access aisle, vertical clearance note (8 feet 2 inches minimum), and signage meeting CBC §11B-502.6. Update the parking plan accordingly.

### Example 11 — Accessible route slope exceeds maximum (Revise)
**Draft input:** Accessible path from parking to entry at 5.5% slope, 3% cross-slope.
**Polished output:** Sheet C-1.0: The accessible route from the accessible parking stall to the primary building entrance has a running slope of 5.5 percent and a cross-slope of 3.0 percent per the civil grading plan. CBC §11B-403.3 limits the running slope of an accessible route to 5.0 percent maximum (ramps per CBC §11B-405), and CBC §11B-403.3 limits cross-slope to 2.0 percent maximum. Revise: regrade the accessible route to achieve a running slope not exceeding 5.0 percent and a cross-slope not exceeding 2.0 percent, or provide a compliant ramp with landings per CBC §11B-405 where the slope must exceed 5 percent, and update the civil grading plan.

## Structural

### Example 12 — Hold-down not specified (Provide)
**Draft input:** Shear wall schedule calls out hold-downs at SW-1 and SW-4 but no product number.
**Polished output:** Sheet S-1.1: The shear wall schedule references hold-down hardware at the end posts of shear wall lines SW-1 and SW-4, but no hold-down product designation, uplift capacity, or ICC-ES evaluation report number is indicated on the structural drawings or specifications. CBC §2308.6.8.1 requires hold-down connectors to be shown on the plans with the type, size, and installation details sufficient for the building official to verify compliance. Provide: specify the hold-down hardware product model number, rated uplift capacity, required anchor bolt size and embedment depth, and the applicable ICC-ES ESR number on the structural plans or schedule.

### Example 13 — Shear wall nailing not specified (Revise)
**Draft input:** SW schedule shows Sheathing type "OSB 15/32" but no nail size or spacing.
**Polished output:** Sheet S-1.1: The shear wall schedule specifies 15/32-inch OSB sheathing at shear wall line SW-2 but does not indicate the required nail size or edge nailing spacing. CBC §2305.3.3 requires shear wall nailing schedules to specify the nail size (diameter and length) and the nail spacing at panel edges and field (intermediate framing members). Revise: add the nail size (e.g., 10d common at 4 inches on-center at edges, 12 inches on-center in field) and panel edge spacing to the shear wall schedule for all shear wall lines. Reference the applicable CBC Table 2306.3.

### Example 14 — Anchor bolt spacing exceeds maximum (Revise)
**Draft input:** Foundation plan shows anchor bolts at 9-foot centers, SDC D project.
**Polished output:** Sheet S-2.1: The foundation plan shows anchor bolts at 9-foot on-center spacing along the perimeter wall. CRC §R403.1.6 requires anchor bolt spacing not to exceed 6 feet on center for sill plates in Seismic Design Category D. Additionally, within 12 inches of each corner and each end of each piece of sill plate, an anchor bolt is required. Revise: revise the foundation plan to show anchor bolts at not more than 6 feet on center with bolts within 12 inches of each plate end and corner. Specify anchor bolt diameter (minimum 1/2 inch), embedment length (minimum 7 inches), and plate washer size (minimum 3 x 3 x 1/4 inch) per CRC §R403.1.6.

## Mechanical

### Example 15 — Combustion air not shown (Provide)
**Draft input:** 100,000 BTUH gas furnace in confined closet, no combustion air openings shown.
**Polished output:** Sheet M-1.0: The plans show a gas-fired furnace rated at 100,000 BTUH in a confined equipment closet of approximately 28 cubic feet, but the plans do not indicate the source, size, or location of combustion air openings. CMC §701.0 and CRC §R303.4.1 require that combustion air be provided to fuel-burning equipment in quantities sufficient for complete combustion, in accordance with the appliance manufacturer's installation instructions and the applicable fuel gas code. Provide: add combustion air opening sizes, locations, and a calculation to the mechanical plans confirming that the minimum required air volume is available. Alternatively, specify a direct-vent or power-vent appliance that does not require indoor combustion air and annotate on the plans.

### Example 16 — Dryer duct exceeds maximum length (Revise)
**Draft input:** Dryer duct shown at 40 feet equivalent length with one 90-degree elbow.
**Polished output:** Sheet M-1.0: The clothes dryer exhaust duct layout shown on the mechanical plan has an equivalent duct length of approximately 40 feet (measured length plus 2.5 feet for the 90-degree elbow), which exceeds the maximum. CMC §504.3.1 and Santa Rosa Building Division Policy BD-M-01 limit the dryer exhaust duct equivalent length to 35 feet. Revise: shorten the duct run to achieve an equivalent length of 35 feet or less, or relocate the dryer to reduce the duct length. Show the revised duct routing with a length calculation table on the mechanical plan. Rigid metallic duct is required for the full run per Santa Rosa local amendment.

### Example 17 — Mechanical ventilation rate not documented (Provide)
**Draft input:** Plans reference "mechanical ventilation" but no CFM rates or system design shown.
**Polished output:** Sheet M-1.0: The plans reference a whole-house mechanical ventilation system but do not indicate the system type (supply-only, exhaust-only, or balanced ERV/HRV), ventilation rate in CFM, or duct layout. CMC §402.0 and Title 24 Part 6 §150.0(o) require that mechanical ventilation be designed to meet ASHRAE Standard 62.2-2019 ventilation rates based on dwelling unit floor area and number of bedrooms. Provide: specify the ventilation system type, rated flow rate, duct diameter, and exterior termination on the mechanical plans. Include a ventilation calculation confirming compliance with ASHRAE 62.2 minimum rates (typically 0.15 CFM/sq ft + 7.5 CFM per bedroom, plus 1 occupant).

## Electrical

### Example 18 — Panel schedule incomplete (Provide)
**Draft input:** Panel schedule shown but lacks conductor sizes and load calculations.
**Polished output:** Sheet E-1.0: The electrical panel schedule shown on Sheet E-1.0 does not include conductor wire sizes (AWG) or calculated loads (watts or volt-amperes) for each circuit. CEC §408.4(A) requires each panelboard to be legibly marked with a circuit directory indicating the purpose and rating of each overcurrent device, and per the Santa Rosa local amendment, the panel schedule shall additionally show conductor size and calculated load per circuit. Provide: complete the panel schedule to include the circuit number, breaker size (amperes), wire size (AWG), load description, and calculated load (watts) for each circuit. Designate circuits reserved for future all-electric appliances per the Santa Rosa local amendment.

### Example 19 — AFCI protection not specified (Revise)
**Draft input:** Bedroom circuits shown without AFCI breaker notation.
**Polished output:** Sheet E-1.0: The panel schedule indicates standard breakers on circuits serving Bedrooms 1, 2, and 3 (circuits 5, 7, and 9) without an AFCI designation. CEC §210.12(A) requires arc-fault circuit interrupter (AFCI) protection for all 120-volt, 15- and 20-ampere circuits in dwelling units, including all bedroom circuits. Revise: update the panel schedule to specify listed combination-type AFCI circuit breakers on all circuits 5, 7, 9, and any other circuits serving habitable rooms. Note the AFCI breaker manufacturer and model on the panel schedule.

### Example 20 — EV charging outlet missing (Provide)
**Draft input:** Garage electrical plan shows no EV circuit.
**Polished output:** Sheet E-1.1: The garage electrical plan does not show the required EV charging outlet. Per the Santa Rosa local amendment to CEC §210.52 (CARP EV readiness mandate), all new single-family dwellings shall provide a minimum dedicated 240-volt, 50-ampere circuit (NEMA 14-50 receptacle or listed EVSE outlet) in the garage or at the primary off-street parking space. Provide: add a dedicated 240-volt, 50-ampere circuit to the garage, show the receptacle location on the electrical plan, and include this circuit on the panel schedule. Specify NEMA 14-50 or listed EVSE outlet type.

## Plumbing

### Example 21 — Water heater seismic strapping not detailed (Provide)
**Draft input:** Plans show 50-gallon gas WH, no seismic strap detail.
**Polished output:** Sheet P-1.0: The plans show a 50-gallon gas-fired water heater but do not include a detail or note for seismic restraint strapping. CPC §507.2 and the Santa Rosa local amendment require water heaters to be anchored or strapped at the upper one-third and lower one-third of the water heater to resist horizontal displacement due to earthquake motion. Provide: add a seismic strapping detail or reference to a standard detail sheet showing the strap size, anchor method, and locations consistent with the California Office of Emergency Services (Cal OES) recommended strap placement. Show a flexible gas connector between the gas shutoff and the water heater appliance inlet.

### Example 22 — Fixture flow rates not shown (Provide)
**Draft input:** Plumbing fixture schedule does not list flow rates.
**Polished output:** Sheet P-1.0: The plumbing fixture schedule lists fixture types and locations but does not specify flow rates for lavatories, kitchen faucets, showerheads, or toilet flush volumes. The Santa Rosa local amendment to CALGreen §4.303.1 requires a WaterSense fixture compliance table on the plumbing plans listing each fixture type, model number, and flow rate (maximum: lavatory 1.2 gpm, kitchen 1.5 gpm, shower 1.8 gpm, toilet 1.28 gpf). Provide: add a WaterSense fixture compliance table to Sheet P-1.0 or the project specifications, identifying each fixture type, the specified model, and confirming compliance with the Santa Rosa maximum flow rate requirements.

### Example 23 — PRV not shown where supply pressure exceeds 80 psi (Provide)
**Draft input:** Civil sheet notes public main pressure at 95 psi; no PRV on plumbing plan.
**Polished output:** Sheet P-1.0: The civil utility plan notes the public water main pressure at the meter as 95 psi. CPC §609.2 and the Santa Rosa local amendment require a pressure-reducing valve (PRV) on all new construction where the domestic water service pressure at the meter exceeds 80 psi. The plumbing plan does not show a PRV on the domestic water service entry. Provide: add a pressure-reducing valve at the domestic water service entry, show the PRV location on the plumbing plan, note the PRV setting (not to exceed 80 psi downstream), and include a detail or manufacturer specification for the listed PRV assembly.

## Energy

### Example 24 — CF-1R not submitted (Provide)
**Draft input:** No Title 24 energy compliance forms in submittal package.
**Polished output:** Submittal-wide: The permit application does not include a Title 24 Part 6 Certificate of Compliance (CF-1R) for the proposed new construction. Title 24 Part 6 §10-103(a)(1) requires that a completed, HERS-registered CF-1R be included with the plans at the time of permit application. Provide: attach a current CalCERTS- or CHEERS-registered CF-1R to the plan set as a named sheet (e.g., T24-1). The CF-1R registration number and registration date shall be printed on the document face.

### Example 25 — Insulation values do not match CF-1R (Revise)
**Draft input:** Plans show R-13 wall insulation but CF-1R shows R-21.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-6.1 / CF-1R: The wall section on Sheet A-6.1 specifies R-13 batt insulation in the 2x4 exterior wall cavity, but the CF-1R dated January 2023 (CalCERTS Registration No. E2023-XXXXX) shows R-21 wall cavity insulation as the compliance path. Title 24 Part 6 §10-103(a) and the CF-1R compliance assumptions require that the plans match the insulation values used in the energy model. Revise: update the wall section and insulation schedule on the architectural plans to match the CF-1R-specified R-21 insulation assembly (or revise the CF-1R energy model to reflect R-13 and confirm continued compliance), and ensure all sheets are consistent.

### Example 26 — Solar readiness conduit missing (Provide)
**Draft input:** New SFR, no solar conduit or panel-dedicated circuit shown.
**Polished output:** Sheet E-1.0: The electrical plans for the new single-family dwelling do not show solar (PV) ready conduit runs from the main electrical panel to the roof per the solar ready requirements. Title 24 Part 6 §110.10(a) requires new single-family dwellings to be solar-zone ready and include a dedicated conduit pathway (minimum 1-inch diameter) and reserved breaker space at the main panel for future PV connection. Provide: add a note and conduit routing on the electrical plan showing a minimum 1-inch conduit from the main panel to the attic and then to the designated solar zone on the roof. Label the reserved breaker space in the panel schedule as "Solar PV — Future."

## Fire/Life Safety

### Example 27 — Smoke alarm location missing (Provide)
**Draft input:** Floor plan does not show smoke alarm symbols.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.1: The floor plan does not indicate the location of smoke alarms in any of the sleeping rooms, outside the sleeping areas, or on each additional story of the dwelling. CRC §R314.3 requires smoke alarms to be installed inside each sleeping room, outside each sleeping room on the same floor, on each additional story (including the basement), and in the path of travel to the sleeping area where a hallway is present. Provide: show the location of all required smoke alarms on the floor plan and first-floor plan using the appropriate electrical symbol (or keynote). Include a note specifying hard-wired, interconnected smoke alarms with battery backup per CRC §R314.4 and §R314.5.

### Example 28 — ADU sprinkler not addressed (Provide)
**Draft input:** 650 sqft ADU shown, no sprinkler system shown or referenced.
**Polished output:** Sheet A-1.1 (ADU): The plans show a proposed 650-square-foot Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) but do not address automatic fire sprinkler requirements. The Santa Rosa local amendment to CRC §R313.1 (SRFD Memo 2020-03) requires an NFPA 13D automatic fire sprinkler system in all new ADUs exceeding 500 square feet. The proposed ADU at 650 square feet exceeds this threshold. Provide: either submit NFPA 13D sprinkler system plans for the ADU concurrently with the building permit application (requires SRFD concurrent review), or submit a deferred-submittal notation on the plans with SRFD pre-approval. Sprinkler system shall be approved by the Santa Rosa Fire Department before frame inspection.

### Example 29 — WUI ignition-resistant construction not called out (Revise)
**Draft input:** Property in Fire Zone 3; no Chapter 7A details on plans.
**Polished output:** Sheets A-1.1, A-6.1: The SRFD Fire Zone 3 designation letter in the submittal confirms this property is located within Santa Rosa Fire Zone 3. However, the architectural plans do not include Chapter 7A Wildland-Urban Interface ignition-resistant construction details for the exterior wall covering, roof covering, soffits, vents, and openings as required by the Santa Rosa local amendment to CBC §R337.1. Revise: add Chapter 7A compliance details to the architectural plans covering: (1) ignition-resistant exterior wall covering per CBC §707A.3; (2) Class A roof covering per CBC §707A.8; (3) ember- and flame-resistant vents per CBC §708A; (4) dual-pane or tempered glazing per CBC §705.8 Santa Rosa local amendment; and (5) signed SRFD Form WUI-01 acknowledgment attached to the building permit application.

## CalGreen

### Example 30 — CWMP not submitted (Provide)
**Draft input:** No Construction Waste Management Plan in submittal.
**Polished output:** Submittal-wide: The permit application does not include a Construction Waste Management Plan (CWMP). CALGreen §4.408.1 and the Santa Rosa local amendment require a completed CWMP to be submitted with the permit application for all new residential construction and additions exceeding 1,000 sq ft. The CWMP shall demonstrate that a minimum of 65 percent of construction waste will be diverted from landfill, identify the intended diversion methods, and name at least two local certified C&D recycling facilities within 20 miles of the project site per Santa Rosa local requirements. Provide: submit a completed CWMP on the approved Santa Rosa form (available at srcity.org/building) prior to permit issuance. A post-construction Diversion Report is required before final inspection.

### Example 31 — CALGreen Tier 1 checklist missing (Provide)
**Draft input:** No CALGreen Tier 1 checklist in application package.
**Polished output:** Submittal-wide: The permit application does not include the CALGreen Tier 1 compliance checklist required for all new single-family residential construction in Santa Rosa. Per the Santa Rosa local amendment to CALGreen §4.201.1, CALGreen Tier 1 measures are mandatory (not voluntary) for new construction. The completed and signed Tier 1 checklist (available on the Santa Rosa Building Division website) shall be submitted with the permit application. Provide: complete and sign the Santa Rosa CALGreen Tier 1 checklist and attach it to the permit application. Key mandatory Tier 1 items include enhanced water conservation fixtures, indoor air quality measures, and the 65 percent waste diversion requirement.

### Example 32 — Electric vehicle charging not included in CalGreen compliance (Provide)
**Draft input:** CalGreen checklist submitted but EV charger item left blank.
**Polished output:** Sheet E-1.0 / CalGreen Checklist Item 4.106.4: The submitted CALGreen checklist does not indicate compliance with the EV charging infrastructure requirement. CALGreen §4.106.4 (as adopted by the 2022 California Title 24) requires new single-family residences to provide electrical infrastructure for EV charging. Additionally, the Santa Rosa local amendment to CEC §210.52 (CARP EV readiness mandate) requires a dedicated 240-volt, 50-ampere circuit in the garage or at the primary parking space. Provide: confirm compliance on the CalGreen checklist and show the EV-ready circuit on the electrical plans per Example 20 above. A NEMA 14-50 outlet or listed EVSE shall be provided.

## Plan Integrity

### Example 33 — Sheet index does not match actual sheets (Clarify)
**Draft input:** Sheet index lists A-6.1 "Wall Sections" but no such sheet in package.
**Polished output:** Title Sheet / Sheet Index: The sheet index on the title sheet lists Sheet A-6.1 "Exterior Wall Sections and Details," but this sheet is not included in the submitted plan set. Conversely, a sheet labeled "A-6.0 Wall Details" is present but is not listed in the sheet index. Clarify: revise the sheet index to accurately reflect all sheets included in the plan set, ensure all referenced sheets are present in the submittal, and confirm the sheet numbering is consistent throughout the plan set including all cross-references and detail callouts.

### Example 34 — Dimension conflict between floor plan and site plan (Clarify)
**Draft input:** Building footprint 42 ft wide on floor plan vs. 38 ft wide on site plan.
**Polished output:** Sheets A-1.1 and C-1.0: The building footprint on the architectural floor plan (Sheet A-1.1) shows a total exterior wall dimension of 42 feet 0 inches in the east-west direction. The site plan (Sheet C-1.0) shows the same building with a footprint dimension of 38 feet 0 inches in the same direction. These two sheets conflict and create an inconsistency in the setback calculations shown on the site plan. Clarify: reconcile the building footprint dimensions between the floor plan and site plan. Confirm the correct exterior dimension and revise both sheets to show consistent values. Update the setback dimensions on the site plan if the building width changes.

### Example 35 — Permit number not shown on all sheets (Provide)
**Draft input:** Permit number shown only on title sheet; missing from all remaining sheets.
**Polished output:** Title Sheet / All Sheets: The Santa Rosa Building Division permit number and plan check case number are shown only on the title sheet and are missing from all remaining sheets (A-1.1 through E-1.1). Santa Rosa Building Division Policy requires the permit number and plan check case number to appear in the title block of every sheet of the plan set. Provide: add the permit number and plan check case number to the title block of all sheets. If a revision cloud is used to identify revisions, the revision date and revision number shall also appear on each revised sheet.
